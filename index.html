Importance Of HEART

The heart is a crucial organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body, ensuring the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to tissues while removing waste products like carbon dioxide.

It operates through a system of rhythmic contractions that maintain circulation, allowing vital organs to function properly.

The heart also helps regulate blood pressure and plays a key role in maintaining overall health by supporting the immune system and facilitating the transport of hormones.

Without the heart, the body would be unable to sustain life, as cells depend on a constant supply of oxygenated blood to survive and function.

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) is a condition where the coronary arteries become narrowed or blocked due to plaque buildup. This restricts blood flow to the heart muscle, potentially leading to chest pain, heart attacks, or other serious complications. Risk factors include high cholesterol, smoking, and high blood pressure.

  • Chest pain or discomfort (angina)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Nausea
  • Symptoms may vary and sometimes can be subtle, especially in women and older adults. Some individuals may not experience symptoms until a heart attack occurs.

Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)

A heart attack, or myocardial infarction, occurs when blood flow to a part of the heart muscle is blocked for an extended period, usually due to a blood clot. This blockage causes damage to the heart muscle, leading to chest pain, shortness of breath, and potential long-term heart damage.

  • Cold sweat
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Shortness of breath
  • Lightheadedness or fainting
  • Pain or discomfort in the arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
  • Severe chest pain or pressure, often described as a squeezing or heavy feeling

Arrhythmias

Arrhythmias are abnormal heart rhythms caused by irregular electrical impulses in the heart. They can lead to the heart beating too fast (tachycardia), too slow (bradycardia), or irregularly. Symptoms may include palpitations, dizziness, shortness of breath, and fatigue. Some arrhythmias may be harmless, while others require medical treatment

  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Fainting or near-fainting episodes
  • Palpitations (feeling a rapid or irregular heartbeat)

Cardiomyopathy

Cardiomyopathy is a disease of the heart muscle that impairs its ability to pump blood effectively. It can be caused by genetic factors, infections, or toxins and may lead to heart failure, arrhythmias, and other complications. Symptoms include shortness of breath, fatigue, and swelling in the legs and abdomen.

  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Swelling in the legs, ankles, or feet

Other Noteworthy Diseases

  • Congenital Heart Defects

    Congenital heart defects are structural abnormalities of the heart present at birth. They can affect heart walls, valves, or vessels, leading to issues with blood flow and causing symptoms like cyanosis or heart murmurs.

    • Cyanosis (bluish skin or lips)
    • Poor feeding or weight gain in infants
    • Heart murmurs or irregular heartbeats

  • Valvular Heart Disease

    Valvular heart disease involves malfunctioning heart valves, causing impaired blood flow. It can lead to symptoms like shortness of breath, fatigue, and heart murmurs. Treatment varies from medication to surgery.

    • Chest pain
    • Fatigue
    • Shortness of breath
    • Persistent cough, especially when lying down
